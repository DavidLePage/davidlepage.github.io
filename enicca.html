Enicca

As we listened to Enicca outside her tiny shack on the stony hillside above Burgersfort her rapist walked behind us, under the low thorn-tender trees. I felt no chill, noted nothing strange in the honeyed afternoon. It’s happened many times before, this passing by of men who rip and tear women; this once, I knew. Rapists are, after all, almost ordinary boyfriends, husbands, fathers, brothers, friends, sometimes strangers. Odds aren’t bad they’re you. For a moment, Enicca’s composure wavered – just a moment – then she spoke again, strong and true.
