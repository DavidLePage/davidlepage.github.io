The Western torment of Gaza

November 29, 2023

Reading Guardian columnist Nesrine Malik's latest column, "The war in Gaza has been an intense lesson in western hypocrisy. It won’t be forgotten"; and seeing the unabashed support of the US, UK and EU for the ongoing brutality in Palestine over the last few weeks has left me feeling that South Africa’s own democracy, for all our flaws, is all the more fragile and precious a fluke of history.

We tend to forget that the apartheid regime, like the apartheid regime in Israel, was propped up by the West for decades before it moved first to a slow and reluctant condemnation of apartheid and then to post-transition self-congratulation. After all, the pillars of “the West” include all too many countries that engaged in colonialism and genocide themselves until the middle of the last century.1

Now we see genocidal levels of killing by Israel – of an order that would never be tolerated for an anti-terrorist operation in New York, Berlin or London – given free license by these Western powers, who parade the fiction that they have no real control over Israeli actions when they could pull the plug on the arms and financial pipelines to Israel very quickly if they wanted to, as sanctions on Russia demonstrate; even quicker, because Russia was not receiving direct “aid”.

Western politicians and media are blind to their own racism because they think or pretend to think that racism is all about crude insults and outlandish bigotry, and pretend not to understand, that racism is also just letting certain categories of people, usually darker and poorer – quietly slide into the “really not very important” zone of your moral consciousness. We see this right now as the genuinely heartbreaking stories of Israeli hostages are given prominence again and again over the equally or even more heartbreaking stories of Palestinians illegally detained for years in far greater numbers and for far longer, supposedly for the “crimes” of resisting occupation.

Self-defence? There are two points to make here:

Firstly, Israel is not in this instance entitled to “self-defence” – it’s not self-defence when you’re an already an occupying power, and the blockade of Gaza makes it a de facto occupier – an occupier that should under the rules of war be actively caring for the needs of the occupied and captive population.

Secondly, it seems the true meaning of “self-defence” has been utterly contaminated, in the public mind by the supposedly “pre-emptive” US-led invasions of Iraq and Afghanistan, because there’s all too little questioning of Israel’s chosen mode of “self-defence”. But self-defence used to mean preparing to fend off a possible or visible imminent attack; not invading and obliterating a territory that contains possible future foes.

Reasonable self-defence in this instance might include rebuilding the barriers between Gaza and southern Israel, restoring and reinforcing the garrisons that were neglected, ensuring improved surveillance.

But however confused the media/public may be, it’s clear the Western powers, Nato, Joe Biden – remain super-clear about the real nature of self-defence – they’ve shown this as, faced with a stronger power, they repeatedly sought to avoid assisting Ukraine with any attacks on Russia that enter its own territory; rather like people who poke a bear on their neighbour’s turf, then run away and leave the neighbours to deal with the consequences.

The Palestinians, being weak, are not afforded equivalent self-restraint: they don’t get the “excuse me, sir; I won’t trouble your nuclear arsenal” version of self-defence that the US/NATO affords Russia.

Israel’s actions are not self-defence. This is locking people up in a prison, and then bombing the prison, and it’s obscene. Israelis, like everyone, are entitled to peace and security – but not at any cost.

There’s another whole layer, unfortunately, to Western hypocrisy here – because not only are they green-lighting genocidal levels of killing under the guise of “self-defence” but because their proposed “post-conflict” solution is to hand Gaza over to the administration of the Palestinian Authority – an entity that is undemocratic, corrupt, brutal and illegitimate.
